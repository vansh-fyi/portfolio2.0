# Case Study: Synofin LMS – The Art of Adaptation

## Key Achievements & Contributions
- **Enterprise Customization**: Successfully adapted a massive Loan Management System for a major client (CSL Finance), replacing an established competitor.
- **Data Standardization**: Created a new design standard for Data Tables, improving readability for high-density financial information.
- **Form Optimization**: Redesigned complex Loan Origination forms to reduce friction and speed up data entry.
- **Strategic UX**: Implemented "Cognitive Ease" by aligning UI patterns with the client's previous tool (Finova) to minimize migration anxiety.

## Process
*See Section 3: The Process below*

## 1. Context: The High-Stakes Migration
While Synovein was a "Build from Scratch" project, **Synofin LMS** was a "Customize and conquer" mission.
* **The Product:** Synofin is Synoriq’s flagship Loan Management System (LMS)—a mature, full-fledged platform.
* **The Mission:** To adapt this massive platform for a major new client (**CSL Finance**) and successfully migrate them away from a leading competitor, **Finova**.
* **The Team:** Vansh worked alongside a Product Manager and another Junior Designer, stationed on-site at the client's office in Noida.

## 2. The Challenge: Beating "Finova"
The primary hurdle was not just functionality, but **User Habit**.
* **The Incumbent:** The client’s employees were heavy users of *Finova*, known for its modern and efficient interface.
* **The Friction:** Moving to a new software usually causes a dip in productivity and user frustration.
* **The Goal:** The new design needed to feel *just as intuitive* as the old one, while offering the superior backend capabilities of Synofin.

## 3. The Process: The Daily Agile Loop


[Image of agile feedback loop diagram]

Working on-site created a hyper-accelerated feedback loop.
* **The Morning Pitch:** Every morning, the PM presented the latest prototype to the **Head of the NBFC**.
* **The Pain Points:** She would voice specific frustrations or features her team missed from their old tool.
* **The Sprint:** Vansh and his co-designer had a strict 9-to-5 window to interpret this feedback, brainstorm user-friendly alternatives, and implement them into the prototype for the next day's pitch.

## 4. Design Execution

### A. Strategic Familiarity (Jakob’s Law)
Vansh employed a strategy of **Cognitive Ease**.
* Instead of forcing a completely new navigation or workflow on the users, he strategically adjusted UI elements to feel **familiar** to the Finova platform they were used to.
* This reduced the learning curve, ensuring that the switch to Synofin felt like an "upgrade" rather than a "restart."

### B. Taming the Data Tables
The existing Synofin platform suffered from inconsistent data presentation.
* **The Fix:** Vansh brought order to the chaos by designing new standards for **Data Tables**.
* **Details:** He defined strict rules for alignment, cell spacing, and typography hierarchies. This made the dense financial data scannable and improved readability for loan officers processing hundreds of files.

### C. Form Flow Optimization
* **The Problem:** Loan origination involves massive forms.
* **The Solution:** Vansh redesigned the form flows to reduce friction, grouping related fields logically and improving input interactions to speed up data entry.

## 5. Outcome & Learnings
* **Deliverables:** A fully customized version of Synofin LMS, tailored to the client's specific workflows, along with an updated **Design System** for tables and forms.
* **The "Masterclass":** This project taught Vansh the nuance of **Enterprise Customization**. He learned that in B2B design, "innovation" sometimes takes a backseat to "familiarity" and "efficiency."
